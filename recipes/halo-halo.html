A vibrant Filipino treat featuring shaved ice, sweet beans, fruits, jellies, and often topped with leche flan and ube halaya.

A popular Filipino shaved ice dessert from Quezon City, Metro Manila, featuring a colorful mix of sweet beans, fruits, jellies, leche flan, ube halaya, and often topped with ube ice cream and pinipig. Meaning "mix-mix," it's a refreshing and customizable treat perfect for the local climate.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup finely shaved ice
  • 2 tablespoons boiled sweet beans (kidney beans, garbanzos, or a mix)
  • 2 tablespoons sweetened kaong (sugar palm fruit), drained
  • 2 tablespoons nata de coco (coconut gel), drained and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon macapuno (sweetened coconut strings), drained
  • 1 tablespoon sweetened saba bananas (plantains), sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ube halaya (purple yam jam)
  • 1 slice leche flan (caramel custard)
  • 1 scoop ube ice cream (purple yam ice cream)
  • 2-3 tablespoons evaporated milk
  • 1 tablespoon pinipig (toasted young rice flakes), optional

Steps:

  1. Sweet ingredients in a tall glass.
  2. Layer sweet ingredients in a tall glass.
  3. Add ube halaya.
  4. Drizzle with evaporated milk.
  5. Top with ube halaya, leche flan, and ube ice cream.
  6. Sprinkle with pinipig (optional).
  7. Serve immediately and mix!
