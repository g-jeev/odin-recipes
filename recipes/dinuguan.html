Dinuguan is a savory Filipino stew made with pork blood, meat, garlic, onions, and vinegar, creating a rich, dark, and tangy flavor. Often served with rice or puto, it's a unique and hearty dish in Filipino cuisine.

Dinuguan is a distinctive Filipino stew with a rich, dark gravy made from pork blood, combined with tender pork and a tangy, slightly spicy flavor.

  • 1.5 lbs pork belly or shoulder, cubed (about 1 inch)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup pork blood (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 tablespoon macapuno (sweetened coconut strings), drained
  • 1-3 Thai chili peppers (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust to your spice preference)/li>
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Steps:

  1. Sweet ingredients in a tall glass.
  2. Layer sweet ingredients in a tall glass.
  3. Add ube halaya.
  4. Drizzle with evaporated milk.
  5. Top with ube halaya, leche flan, and ube ice cream.
  6. Sprinkle with pinipig (optional).
  7. Serve immediately and mix!
